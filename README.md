# SQL-Project-1
Nova Retail Group - SQL Data Analysis
## Project Overview
This project looks at sales data from Nova Retail Group, a retail company operating across South Africa. The aim was to understand how the business is performing in terms of sales, customers and products and to turn that data into useful insights for decision-making.
## The analysis is based on four tables:
Products – details like price and cost.  
Customers – who the customers are and where they are from.  
Sales – all transaction records.  
CustomerFeedback – ratings and customer satisfaction. 
 ## What I Did
I worked through the data step by step:

Checked and explored the data to understand what I was working with.  
Joined tables using SQL to connect customers, products and sales  
Used aggreagate functions to find solutions  
Looked at trends across time, regions and sales channels.  
Applied more advanced SQL techniques like CTEs and window functions.  
## Findings
Customers spend a lot per purchase, with an average order value of 6,751.33, indicating strong transaction value.

Orders with no discount generate higher profit margins compared to orders with very high discounts.

Customers who are more satisfied (ratings 4–5) tend to buy again more often than less satisfied ones. This shows that happy customers are more likely to come back.

Higher-performing products generate more revenue and sales volume, while lower-performing ones contribute less despite similar profit margins and average ratings.

The business demonstrates strong performance overall, with high total revenue, a healthy profit margin, consistent order activity and a solid customer base, indicating stable and efficient operations.

## This is what it means for the business
Encourage higher spending through bundles, cross-selling, and loyalty rewards.

Reduce heavy discounting to protect and improve profit margins.

Improve customer experience to increase repeat purchases and retention.

Focus on strong products and review or improve weaker ones

## Skills used
SQL (basic to advanced)

Business thinking with data.

This project shows how raw data can be turned into simple, useful business insights. It is not just about writing SQL, it is about understanding what the data is saying and using it to make better decisions.
